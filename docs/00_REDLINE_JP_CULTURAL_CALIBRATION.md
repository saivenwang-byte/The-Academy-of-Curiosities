# Japan Cultural Calibration Redlines

Status: ACTIVE  
Priority: HIGHEST PROJECT RULE  
Mandatory Advisor: 田中みどり — 日本校园文化顾问

## Core rule

All materials for 学堂趣事录 must be reviewed by the Japan Cultural Calibration Agent before sample production.

Do not translate Chinese school memory directly into Japanese school life. Use contemporary Japanese elementary and junior high school settings, vocabulary, routines, spaces, and cultural details.

The project must make Japanese readers ages 6–14 feel: this story could happen in a school I know.

## Agent panels to use

1. Cultural Calibration: paste draft text and scan for cultural mismatch.
2. Five-layer Sensory System: inspect setting through school system, space and objects, social rules, child subculture, and emotional distance.
3. School Glossary: use Japanese campus words with lived context, not dictionary translation.

## Mandatory five layers

1. System and routine: opening ceremonies, school lunch duty, class roles, club or committee activities, after-school timing.
2. Space and objects: uwabaki, getabako, classroom sliding doors, blackboard area, toilets, school bags, desks, shoe lockers, cleaning tools.
3. Social rules: senpai-kohai where relevant, reading the room, quiet group pressure, class atmosphere.
4. Child culture: randoseru, summer independent research, school mysteries, bag charms, toys hidden in bags.
5. Emotional expression: restrained friendship, walking home together, saying jaa ne at the corner, praising effort with ganbatta ne.

## Current high-risk items

- Do not write a student entering a classroom without first considering the shoe-changing route: school entrance, shoe locker, and uwabaki.
- Do not write a hinged classroom door by default. Japanese classroom doors are often sliding doors; verify the specific building.
- Do not use Chinese class-monitor or class-cadre logic without converting it into Japanese class roles, duty systems, committees, or day-duty roles.
- Do not use a Chinese-style cafeteria scene for elementary school lunch. Check classroom lunch and school lunch duty first.
- Do not let elementary school children remain after school without a plausible reason such as class role work, committee work, library duty, cleaning duty, teacher permission, after-school care, or a school program.
- Do not add Japanese flavor by simply adding shrine gates, kimono, festivals, cherry blossoms, or folklore. Use them only when the place, season, and story logic require them.

## First-volume warning: The Always-Wet Chair

Before drafting, define:

- school stage and grade
- month and weather
- exact school space
- why the children are there after class
- chair material and seat surface
- nearest window, sink, cleaning cabinet, shoe route, lunch route, or pool route
- possible everyday causes before any mystery framing

The story should begin as a small school mystery and end as a child-verifiable observation or experiment.

## Workflow

Draft -> Japan Cultural Calibration Agent -> revise red warnings -> editorial review -> archive.

No draft can be marked READY_FOR_SAMPLE, READY_FOR_TRANSLATION, or READY_FOR_ARTIST unless it has passed the Agent review.
